Concept: Merge Two Sorted Arrays

This introduces another important application of the Two Pointers technique.

Imagine you have:

arr1 = [1,3,5]
arr2 = [2,4,6]

Both arrays are already sorted.

Your task is to merge them into one sorted array:

[1,2,3,4,5,6]
Why Two Pointers?

Keep one pointer for each array:

arr1: 1 3 5
      ↑

arr2: 2 4 6
      ↑

Compare:

1 vs 2

Take the smaller one (1) and move the first pointer.

Now compare:

3 vs 2

Take 2 and move the second pointer.

Repeat until one array finishes, then append the remaining elements.

Algorithm
Create an empty result array.
Set i = 0 for arr1 and j = 0 for arr2.
While both arrays have elements:
Compare arr1[i] and arr2[j].
Append the smaller value.
Move the corresponding pointer.
Append any remaining elements from either array.
Return the merged array.
