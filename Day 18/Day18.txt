Concept: Sorting (Bubble Sort)

Before learning advanced sorts, we need to understand why sorting works.

What is Sorting?

Input:

[5,2,4,1,3]

Output:

[1,2,3,4,5]
Bubble Sort Idea

Compare adjacent elements.

If wrong order:

Swap

Example:

5 2 4 1 3

Compare:

5 and 2

Swap:

2 5 4 1 3

Compare:

5 and 4

Swap:

2 4 5 1 3

Compare:

5 and 1

Swap:

2 4 1 5 3

Compare:

5 and 3

Swap:

2 4 1 3 5

Notice:

Largest element reached the end.

Like a bubble floating upward.

Hence:

Bubble Sort
Bubble Sort Code
def bubble_sort(arr):

    n=len(arr)

    for i in range(n):

        for j in range(0,n-i-1):

            if arr[j] > arr[j+1]:

                arr[j],arr[j+1]=arr[j+1],arr[j]

    return arr
Time Complexity

Two nested loops:

O(n²)

Not efficient.

But very important for learning sorting.
