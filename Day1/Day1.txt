Part 5: First Logic Problem (20 min)
Find the Larger Number

Take two numbers.

Input:

10
20

Output:

20 is larger
Think Before Coding

Ask yourself:

What are the inputs?
Two numbers
What is the output?
Larger number
How do humans solve it?
Compare both numbers

Now code:

a = int(input())
b = int(input())

if a > b:
    print(a, "is larger")
else:
    print(b, "is larger")
Part 6: Brainstorming Exercise

Don't code immediately.

Problem:

Find the largest among 3 numbers.

Example:

5 8 3

Output:

8
First write in English:

Example:

1. Take three numbers.
2. Assume first number is largest.
3. Compare with second.
4. Compare with third.
5. Print largest.

Only after writing steps should you code.

This habit is what builds logic.

Brain Exercise

Before writing code, write the algorithm in English.

Example:

Problem: Even or Odd

Step 1: Take a number.
Step 2: Divide by 2.
Step 3: Check remainder.
Step 4: If remainder is 0, even.
Step 5: Otherwise odd.
One rule for your DSA journey

For every problem:

Understand Problem
↓
Solve Manually
↓
Write Steps in English
↓

Test

Don't look at solutions for at least 15 minutes when you're stuck. Struggling is where logic building happens.
